Gulab jamun recipe

Gulab Jamun is a classic and beloved Indian dessert renowned for its soft texture and sweet, aromatic flavor. These delectable sweet treats consist of round, doughnut-like balls made from khoya (milk solids) or milk powder, flour, and a leavening agent such as baking powder. The dough is gently kneaded and rolled into small, smooth balls, which are then deep-fried until they achieve a golden-brown color. Once fried, the Gulab Jamun balls are soaked in a fragrant sugar syrup infused with cardamom and often flavored with rose water, imparting a delightful floral essence. This syrup bathes the Gulab Jamun, infusing them with sweetness and moisture, ensuring each bite is bursting with flavor and tenderness. Gulab Jamun is a staple dessert at festive occasions such as weddings, celebrations, and religious festivals like Diwali and Eid. It is typically served warm, allowing the syrup to envelop the soft balls, creating a heavenly indulgence that delights the senses. Whether enjoyed on its own or paired with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream, Gulab Jamun is a timeless dessert that never fails to satisfy the sweet tooth and leave a lasting impression with its irresistible taste and decadent texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or melted butter
  • 3-4 tablespoons milk (or as needed to make a soft dough)
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For the sugar syrup:
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon rose water
  • Saffron strands (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking powder. Mix well.
  2. Add ghee or melted butter to the dry ingredients and mix until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add milk, a little at a time, and knead into a soft dough. The dough should be smooth and pliable.
  4. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls and roll them between your palms to make smooth balls. Make sure there are no cracks.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, reduce the heat to low and carefully add the dough balls to the oil.
  6. Fry the balls on low heat, stirring gently and continuously, until they turn golden brown and evenly cooked from all sides. This may take about 8-10 minutes.
  7. While the balls are frying, prepare the sugar syrup. In a separate saucepan, combine sugar, water, and crushed cardamom pods. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the syrup slightly thickens.
  8. Once the Gulab Jamun balls are golden brown, remove them from the oil and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  9. Immediately transfer the hot Gulab Jamun balls into the warm sugar syrup. Allow them to soak in the syrup for at least 1-2 hours to absorb the sweetness and flavor.
  10. Garnish with saffron strands (if using) and serve Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature.
